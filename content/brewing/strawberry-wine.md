steps:
  - title: "Prepare the Strawberries"
    description: "Start by pureeing 5.76 lbs of fresh strawberries. The pureeing process breaks down the fruit cell walls, releasing more juice and flavor compounds than using whole berries."
    image: /images/content/brewing/strawberry-wine/step-1.jpg
  
  - title: "Add Camden Tablet Treatment"
    description: "Place the pureed strawberries into a brew bag and add one crushed Camden tablet. The Camden tablet contains potassium metabisulfite, which eliminates wild yeasts and bacteria present on the fruit. This is a critical step for strawberry wine — strawberries carry a lot of wild yeast that can compete with your chosen yeast strain or produce off-flavors. Let the strawberries sit with the Camden tablet for 2 days before proceeding."
    image: /images/content/brewing/strawberry-wine/step-3.jpg
  
  - title: "Create the Must"
    description: "After the 2-day waiting period, add the strawberry-filled brew bag to your fermenter. Add 1.5 lbs of granulated sugar and fill with water to the top of the fermenter. Stir gently but thoroughly until all sugar crystals have dissolved — this takes quite a while, but it's important not to proceed until the sugar is fully incorporated."
  
  - title: "Pitch the Yeast"
    description: "Once the sugar is completely dissolved, pitch one packet of Red Star Premier Cote Des Blancs yeast. This yeast strain is ideal for fruit wines — it ferments cleanly while preserving the delicate strawberry aromatics. Install the airlock and let fermentation begin."
  
  - title: "Primary Fermentation"
    description: "Allow primary fermentation to go for as long as necessary until there is no more activity in your airlock. Note that this tends to take between 2 to 3 weeks, but you can check with a triple scale hydrometer to ensure fermentation is complete. After two weeks, remove the strawberry purée bag from the must and allow fermentation to continue until complete. Starting OG was 1.080."
  
  - title: "Transfer to Secondary"
    description: "My final gravity after primary was 0.994. Transfer the wine to secondary fermentation, leaving any sediment behind. Conditioning began after the secondary phase."
  
  - title: "Bulk Conditioning"
    description: "After secondary fermentation completed, I let the wine condition in bulk for 3.5 months before bottling. During this time it cleared up drastically — what started as a cloudy, pink liquid settled into a beautifully clear, bright strawberry wine."
  
  - title: "Bottling"
    description: "After four months of fermentation and aging, bottle the wine. The long secondary period allowed the wine to clear and develop complexity."
    image: /images/content/brewing/strawberry-wine/step-bottling.jpg
